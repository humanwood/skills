# Learning Contexts Reference

## Learner Types

### üéØ Exam Prep
**Goal:** Pass high-stakes test
**Approach:**
- Question bank practice (thousands of questions)
- Weak area targeting
- Timed simulations
- Pattern recognition

**AI role:** Generate questions, explain wrong answers, track weak areas

### üîß Skill Acquisition
**Goal:** Be able to DO something
**Approach:**
- Deliberate practice
- Immediate feedback
- Progressive difficulty
- Real application

**AI role:** Design practice, provide feedback, decompose skills

### üìö Academic/Research
**Goal:** Deep understanding of field
**Approach:**
- Literature synthesis
- Critical reading
- Mental model building
- Connection across papers

**AI role:** Summarize, compare, surface gaps, challenge assumptions

### ‚ö° Practical/Just-in-Time
**Goal:** Learn enough to unblock
**Approach:**
- Learn when needed, not "in case"
- Minimum viable understanding
- Apply immediately

**AI role:** Quick answers, just enough context, point to depth if wanted

### üîç Curiosity-Driven
**Goal:** Understand for its own sake
**Approach:**
- Follow interest
- No fixed curriculum
- Depth varies by fascination

**AI role:** Feed curiosity, make connections, suggest rabbit holes

## Life Stages

| Stage | Key Needs |
|-------|-----------|
| **Children** | Play-based, visual, celebrate questions |
| **Teens** | Respect autonomy, connect to identity |
| **University** | Deep dives, exam support, time management |
| **Professionals** | Get to point, practical, micro-learning |
| **Seniors** | Patient, larger text, connect to experience |

## Adapting AI Behavior

Detect learner type from:
- Explicit statement ("I have an exam")
- Question style (specific vs exploratory)
- Time pressure signals
- Domain context

Adapt:
- Depth of explanation
- Speed of delivery
- Amount of scaffolding
- Practice style
