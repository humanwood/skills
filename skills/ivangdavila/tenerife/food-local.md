# Canarian Cuisine — Local Food Guide

## The Essentials

### Papas Arrugadas con Mojo
The dish that defines Canarian cuisine.

**The potatoes**:
- Small waxy potatoes (papas bonitas)
- Boiled in heavily salted water
- Water evaporates, salt crystallizes on skin
- Wrinkled texture, intensely flavored

**Mojo rojo** (red sauce):
- Garlic, cumin, paprika, vinegar, oil
- Ranges from mild to very spicy (picón)
- Goes with potatoes, meat, bread

**Mojo verde** (green sauce):
- Cilantro or parsley base
- Garlic, cumin, vinegar, oil
- Traditionally for fish

**Where**: Everywhere, but best at traditional restaurants, not tourist strips.

### Gofio
Pre-Hispanic staple still eaten daily.

**What it is**:
- Toasted grain flour (wheat, corn, or mixed)
- Ground from roasted grains
- Incredibly versatile

**How it's eaten**:
- **Gofio escaldado**: Mixed with fish broth, onion
- **Gofio amasado**: Kneaded with mojo, banana
- **In milk**: Breakfast porridge style
- **Desserts**: Ice cream, mousse

**Where to try**: Local restaurants, especially La Laguna, Puerto de la Cruz.

## Meat Dishes

### Carne Fiesta
The party meat (literally "fiesta meat").
- Pork cubes marinated in garlic, herbs
- Fried until crispy edges
- Essential at any Canarian celebration
- Often in guachinches

### Conejo en Salmorejo
- Rabbit in salmorejo marinade (not the cold soup)
- Garlic, paprika, oregano, vinegar
- Tender, deeply flavored
- Traditional recipe varies by family

### Costillas con Papas y Piña
- Pork ribs slow-cooked
- With potatoes and corn (piña de millo)
- Comfort food classic

### Ropa Vieja
"Old clothes" — shredded meat stew:
- Originally used leftover meat from cocido
- With chickpeas, peppers, onion
- Rich, satisfying

### Puchero Canario
The Canarian stew:
- Multiple meats (chicken, beef, pork)
- Chickpeas, vegetables
- Corn, sweet potato
- Served in courses sometimes
- Sunday family tradition

## Seafood

### Fresh Fish (Pescado Fresco)

| Fish | Description | Best Preparation |
|------|-------------|------------------|
| Vieja | Parrotfish, sweet flesh | Fried or grilled |
| Cherne | Wreckfish, firm white | Baked, in sauce |
| Sama | Sea bream, delicate | Grilled whole |
| Bocinegro | Red porgy, prized | A la espalda |
| Sargo | White seabream | Fried |
| Cabrilla | Comber, tasty | Fried or soup |

**Preparations**:
- **A la espalda**: Butterflied and grilled
- **A la plancha**: Grilled flat
- **Frito**: Fried (usually smaller fish)
- **En escabeche**: Marinated after cooking

### Sancocho Canario
Traditional salted fish dish:
- Salt-cured wreckfish (cherne)
- Desalted and boiled
- Served with sweet potato, gofio, mojo
- Historically for fishermen's families
- Now celebratory (May, especially)

### Pulpo (Octopus)
- Usually boiled and sliced
- Served with papas arrugadas
- Or a la gallega style (paprika, oil)

### Lapas (Limpets)
- Grilled with garlic butter and mojo
- Gathered from rocks
- Delicacy, expensive

### Gambas and Camarones
- Shrimp and prawns
- Al ajillo (garlic), a la plancha
- Fresh from local boats

## Cheese (Queso)

### Tenerife Cheeses
All from local goat milk tradition.

**Queso fresco**: 
- Fresh, mild, soft
- Days old
- Breakfast or with mojo

**Queso semicurado**:
- 2-3 months aged
- Firmer, more flavor
- Most common

**Queso curado**:
- 6+ months aged
- Hard, intense
- Grating or alone

**Queso ahumado** (smoked):
- Traditional smoking method
- Distinctive flavor

### How to Eat
- **Queso asado con mojo**: Grilled, with red or green mojo — essential
- Alone with wine
- On bread for breakfast
- Grated over pasta/potatoes

### Where to Buy
- Mercado de Nuestra Señora de África (Santa Cruz)
- Local markets (La Laguna Sunday)
- Direct from farms (check Anaga, south slopes)

## Sweets & Desserts

### Traditional

**Bienmesabe** ("tastes good to me"):
- Ground almonds, egg yolks, sugar
- Honey, lemon
- Rich, dense
- Often with ice cream

**Frangollo**:
- Corn flour base
- Almonds, raisins, lemon
- Cinnamon topped
- Grandma's dessert

**Quesillo**:
- Canarian flan
- Denser than mainland flan
- Caramel topped

**Truchas de batata**:
- Pastry pockets
- Sweet potato filling
- Christmas traditional
- Also with cabello de ángel (pumpkin threads)

**Príncipe Alberto**:
- Chocolate and almond cake
- Local invention

### Fruits

Tenerife grows amazing tropical fruits:
- **Plátano de Canarias**: Smaller, sweeter than mainland bananas
- **Papaya**: Grows locally, sweet
- **Mango**: Season June-September
- **Chirimoya**: Custard apple, winter
- **Prickly pear (tunos)**: From cactus, summer

## Drinks

### Non-Alcoholic
- **Café cortado**: Espresso with small milk, standard order
- **Barraquito**: Layered coffee with condensed milk, liqueur, lemon
- **Zumo natural**: Fresh juice (naranja, papaya, mango)

### Alcoholic
- **Canarian wine**: See food-practical.md
- **Ron miel**: Honey rum, sweet, digestif
- **Licor de plátano**: Banana liqueur
- **Sangría**: Common in tourist areas
