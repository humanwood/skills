# Cost of Living — Tenerife

## Overview

Tenerife offers excellent value — lower than mainland Spain, significantly lower than Northern Europe.

## Taxes & Contributions

### Income Tax (IRPF)
Progressive rates, slightly lower in Canarias:

| Income | Rate |
|--------|------|
| Up to €12,450 | 18.5% |
| €12,451-20,200 | 24% |
| €20,201-35,200 | 30% |
| €35,201-60,000 | 37% |
| Over €60,000 | 47% |

### Social Security (Autónomo)
- Minimum: ~€300/month
- Income-based system now
- Covers healthcare + pension

### VAT (IGIC)
- Standard: 7% (vs 21% mainland)
- Reduced: 3% (food basics)
- Zero: Some essentials

### Property Tax (IBI)
- Annual, varies by municipality
- €200-800/year typical for apartments

### What Makes It Cheaper
- Lower VAT (7% vs 21%)
- Cheaper fuel
- Affordable rent (outside Costa Adeje)
- Cheap local dining
- Free entertainment (beach, hiking)
- Lower services costs

### What's Comparable/More
- Imported goods (shipping)
- Electronics (similar)
- Quality international restaurants
- Brand-name fashion
