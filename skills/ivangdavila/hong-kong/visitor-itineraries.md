# Hong Kong Itineraries

> Last updated: February 2025

## 1-Day Highlight Tour

### "The Essentials" (10-12 hours)

**Morning (9 AM - 12 PM)**
1. Start at **Central** - coffee at a cha chaan teng (local caf)
2. Take the **Mid-Levels Escalator** up through SoHo
3. Walk to **Man Mo Temple** (Hollywood Road) - Hong Kong's oldest temple
4. Browse **Cat Street** antiques nearby

**Lunch (12-1:30 PM)**
- **Budget:** Dim sum at Lin Heung Tea House (HK$100-150 / $13-19 USD)
- **Mid-range:** Tim Ho Wan (Michelin-starred dim sum, HK$100-200 / $13-26 USD)

**Afternoon (1:30-6 PM)**
1. Take **Star Ferry** to Tsim Sha Tsui (HK$5)
2. Walk the **TST Waterfront Promenade**
3. Quick visit to **Avenue of Stars** (Bruce Lee statue)
4. **Bus 15** (or Peak Tram) to Victoria Peak

**Sunset (6-7:30 PM)**
- Watch sunset from **Lugard Road** viewpoint
- Walk the loop trail (1 hour)

**Evening (7:30 PM onwards)**
1. Return to TST for **Symphony of Lights** (8 PM)
2. Dinner in TST or cross back to Central
3. Optional: Drinks in **Lan Kwai Fong** (Central)

**Budget Version:** ~HK$300-400 ($38-51 USD) including transport and food
**Splurge Version:** ~HK$800-1,000 ($102-128 USD) including nicer restaurants

## 3-Day Comprehensive Itinerary

### Day 1: Hong Kong Island - Heritage & Heights

**Morning**
- 9 AM: Breakfast at local cha chaan teng (try eggs, toast, milk tea)
- 10 AM: **Man Mo Temple** and **Cat Street** (Sheung Wan)
- 11 AM: Walk through **PMQ** creative hub

**Lunch**
- Dim sum at **Yat Lok** or **Kam's Roast Goose** (both Michelin-starred, HK$80-150)

**Afternoon**
- 1:30 PM: Take **ding ding tram** from Sheung Wan to Causeway Bay (45 min ride)
- 2:30 PM: Explore Causeway Bay - **Times Square**, local street markets
- 4:30 PM: **Bus 15** to Victoria Peak

**Evening**
- 5:30 PM: Sunset from the Peak
- 7 PM: Peak Tram down
- 8 PM: Dinner in **SoHo** (international options)
- 10 PM: Drinks at **Lan Kwai Fong**

---

### Day 2: Kowloon - Culture & Markets

**Morning**
- 9 AM: **Chi Lin Nunnery** & **Nan Lian Garden** (Diamond Hill MTR)
  - Allow 2 hours for peaceful exploration
- 11:30 AM: MTR to Mong Kok

**Lunch**
- **Mong Kok dai pai dong** (outdoor food stalls) or noodles shop

**Afternoon**
- 1 PM: **Goldfish Market** and **Flower Market** (Mong Kok)
- 2:30 PM: Walk to **Yau Ma Tei** - old Hong Kong atmosphere
- 3:30 PM: **Hong Kong Museum of History** (HK$10, free Wed)
  - Excellent "Hong Kong Story" permanent exhibition

**Evening**
- 5:30 PM: Walk to TST waterfront
- 7 PM: **Temple Street Night Market** (opens 4 PM, lively by 7 PM)
- 8 PM: Symphony of Lights from waterfront
- 9 PM: Dinner at TST - seafood or local Cantonese

---

### Day 3: Lantau Island - Buddha & Nature

**Full Day Trip**

**Morning**
- 8:30 AM: MTR to **Tung Chung**
- 9 AM: **Ngong Ping 360 Cable Car** (book online to skip queues)
  - 25-minute scenic ride over mountains
- 10 AM: **Big Buddha** (climb 268 steps)
- 10:30 AM: **Po Lin Monastery**
- 11:30 AM: Vegetarian lunch at monastery restaurant

**Afternoon - Choose Your Adventure:**

**Option A: Beach Day**
- Bus to **Cheung Sha Beach** (Hong Kong's longest beach)
- Relax, swim, lunch at beachside restaurants
- Return via bus to Tung Chung

**Option B: Village Walk**
- Walk to **Ngong Ping Village** (tourist shops, skippable)
- Bus 23 down the mountain to **Tai O** fishing village
- Explore stilt houses, dried seafood shops
- Boat tour to see pink dolphins (HK$60)
- Bus back to Tung Chung

**Evening**
- Return to city
- Optional: CityGate Outlets at Tung Chung for shopping

## 7-Day Deep Dive Itinerary

### Days 1-3: Core Hong Kong
Follow the 3-day itinerary above.

---

### Day 4: Macau Day Trip ð

**Getting There**
- **Ferry** from Sheung Wan or TST: HK$170-200 (~$22-26 USD), 1 hour
- **Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge bus**: HK$65 (~$8 USD), 45 min

**Macau Highlights (Full Day)**

**Morning**
- 9 AM: Ferry/bus to Macau
- 10:30 AM: **Ruins of St. Paul's** - iconic facade
- 11 AM: **Senado Square** - Portuguese colonial architecture
- 11:30 AM: **A-Ma Temple** - oldest in Macau

**Lunch**
- Portuguese-Macanese food: African chicken, bacalhau (salted cod)
- Try **Lord Stow's** egg tarts (HK$12 each)

**Afternoon**
- 2 PM: **Cotai Strip** - Venetian, Parisian, Studio City casinos
- Free to walk through and gawk
- 5 PM: **Taipa Village** - charming streets, colonial houses

**Evening**
- 6 PM: Watch sunset from Taipa waterfront
- 7 PM: Return ferry to Hong Kong
- Optional: Stay for casino nightlife

**Budget:** ~HK$400-500 ($51-64 USD) transport + food
**Gambling budget:** Whatever you're willing to lose

---

### Day 5: Outlying Islands

**Choose One:**

#### Option A: Lamma Island (Full Day)
- 9 AM: Ferry from Central Pier 4 to Yung Shue Wan (HK$20, 30 min)
- Morning: Walk the island trail (1.5 hours to Sok Kwu Wan)
- Lunch: Seafood at rainbow restaurants in Sok Kwu Wan
- Afternoon: Beach time at Hung Shing Yeh
- 5 PM: Ferry back from Sok Kwu Wan

#### Option B: Cheung Chau Island (Full Day)
- 9 AM: Ferry from Central Pier 5 (HK$15-30, 35-55 min)
- Morning: Rent a bicycle, explore temples
- Lunch: Fresh seafood, fish balls (famous local snack)
- Afternoon: Tung Wan Beach
- 5 PM: Return ferry

#### Option C: Peng Chau Island (Half Day)
- Tiny, quirky island - car-free, no real attractions
- Old leather factory turned art space
- Perfect for slow afternoon wandering
- 2-3 hours is enough

---

### Day 6: New Territories & Hidden Hong Kong

**Morning: Sham Shui Po**
- 9 AM: **Ex-Sham Shui Po Service Reservoir** (underground heritage)
- 10:30 AM: Walk through **Sham Shui Po** neighborhood
  - Fabric markets, electronics, working-class Hong Kong
- 11:30 AM: **Apliu Street Flea Market** - secondhand electronics, vintage

**Lunch**
- Local noodles or dai pai dong in Sham Shui Po (HK$40-60)

**Afternoon: Sha Tin**
- 1 PM: MTR to Sha Tin
- 1:30 PM: **10,000 Buddhas Monastery** (actually 13,000+ statues)
  - 400+ steps up - bring water
- 3 PM: Walk along **Shing Mun River**
- 4 PM: **Heritage Museum** (if interested in HK history/culture)

**Evening**
- Return to city for dinner
- Happy Valley Horse Races if it's Wednesday night (HK$20 entry)

---

### Day 7: Flexible / Shopping / Recovery

**Option A: Shopping Day**
- **Central**: IFC Mall, Landmark (luxury)
- **Causeway Bay**: Times Square, Hysan Place (mid-range)
- **Mong Kok**: Sneaker Street, electronics
- **CityGate Outlets** (Tung Chung): Discounts

**Option B: Hiking Day**
- **Dragon's Back** trail - easy 8km, stunning coastal views
- **Lion Rock** - more challenging, iconic skyline panorama

**Option C: Relaxation Day**
- Spa treatments in hotel
- Afternoon tea at **Peninsula Hotel** (HK$688, $88 USD - splurge)
- Slow dim sum brunch

## Budget vs Luxury Breakdown

### Budget Traveler (HK$500-800/day, $64-102 USD)

**Accommodation:** Hostel or budget hotel in TST (HK$200-400)
**Food:**
- Breakfast: Cha chaan teng (HK$30-50)
- Lunch: Dai pai dong, noodles (HK$40-60)
- Dinner: Local restaurant (HK$80-120)
**Transport:** Octopus card, buses, trams (HK$30-50)
**Activities:** Free attractions, walking, markets

**Sample Day:**
- Breakfast: Milk tea + toast (HK$35)
- Star Ferry (HK$5)
- Lunch: Dim sum (HK$80)
- Bus to Peak (HK$12)
- Dinner: Local Cantonese (HK$100)
- Drinks: 7-Eleven beer (HK$15)
- **Total: ~HK$247 ($32 USD)**

---

### Mid-Range Traveler (HK$1,500-2,500/day, $192-320 USD)

**Accommodation:** 3-4 star hotel (HK$800-1,200)
**Food:**
- Breakfast: Hotel or nice caf (HK$80-120)
- Lunch: Quality restaurant (HK$150-250)
- Dinner: Good restaurant + drinks (HK$300-500)
**Transport:** MTR, occasional taxi (HK$80-150)
**Activities:** Peak Tram, museums, cable car

---

### Luxury Traveler (HK$5,000+/day, $640+ USD)

**Accommodation:** 5-star hotel (HK$2,500-5,000+)
- Peninsula, Mandarin Oriental, Four Seasons
**Food:**
- Breakfast: Hotel buffet (HK$300-500)
- Lunch: Fine dining (HK$500-1,000)
- Dinner: Michelin restaurant + wine (HK$1,500-3,000+)
**Transport:** Private car, helicopter transfers available
**Activities:**
- Private Peak tour
- Junk boat rental (HK$5,000+ for half day)
- Bespoke experiences

**Splurge-Worthy Experiences:**
- Afternoon tea at Peninsula Hotel (HK$688/person)
- Dinner at top bars (Bar Leone, Argo - HK$500+ for cocktails)
- Helicopter tour (HK$2,000+)
- Private junk party (HK$5,000-15,000 for boat)
