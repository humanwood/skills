# Food Markets — Madrid

## Gastro Markets (Mercados Gastronómicos)

Modern food halls with multiple stalls, designed for eating and drinking rather than grocery shopping.

### Mercado de San Miguel

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Plaza de San Miguel (next to Plaza Mayor) |
| **Hours** | Sun-Wed 10am-12am, Thu-Sat 10am-2am |
| **Vibe** | Very touristy, beautiful building |
| **Price** | €€€ (tourist markup) |

**Honest take:** Stunning iron and glass building (1916). But VERY overpriced and crowded. Worth seeing once for the architecture but locals avoid eating here.

**Best for:** Quick stop for atmosphere, a single tapa
**Skip:** Full meals, wine (marked up 200%)

### Mercado de San Antón

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Chueca (Calle Augusto Figueroa) |
| **Hours** | Daily 10am-midnight (restaurant floor later) |
| **Vibe** | Local-ish, LGBTQ+ friendly |
| **Price** | €€ |

**Three floors:**
- Ground: Traditional market stalls
- First: Gourmet food stalls
- Rooftop: Restaurant with terrace views

**Best for:** Lunch in Chueca, rooftop drinks, actually buying groceries + eating
**Skip if:** Want super authentic traditional experience

### Mercado de San Ildefonso

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Malasaña (Calle Fuencarral) |
| **Hours** | Sun-Wed 12pm-12am, Thu-Sat 12pm-2am |
| **Vibe** | Young, international, street food |
| **Price** | €€ |

**Why go:** Madrid's first "street food market" — 3 floors, 16+ stalls. Great for groups who can't agree on one cuisine.

**Best for:** Groups, variety, casual eating
**Skip if:** Want traditional Spanish

### Mercado de la Cebada

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | La Latina (Plaza de la Cebada) |
| **Hours** | Mon-Sat 9am-2pm, 5pm-8pm |
| **Vibe** | Local, working market |
| **Price** | € |

**Real deal:** Actual neighborhood market. Some stalls now do tapas. Mix of groceries and eating. Feels authentic.

**Best for:** Combining with El Rastro Sunday visit
**Skip if:** Want polished food hall experience

## Traditional Markets (Mercados Tradicionales)

Real markets where madrileños actually shop. Some have eating options.

### Mercado de la Paz

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Salamanca (Calle Ayala) |
| **Hours** | Mon-Fri 9am-8pm, Sat 9am-2:30pm |
| **Vibe** | Upscale neighborhood market |
| **Price** | €€-€€€ |

**Star:** Casa Dani — arguably best tortilla española in Madrid. Worth the trip alone.

**Best for:** High-quality groceries, Casa Dani tortilla
**The tortilla:** Creamy, runny center. Order media (half) unless very hungry.

### Mercado de Antón Martín

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Lavapiés/Huertas border |
| **Hours** | Mon-Fri 9am-9pm, Sat 9am-3pm |
| **Vibe** | Multicultural, local |
| **Price** | €-€€ |

**Why special:** Mix of traditional Spanish + international (Peruvian, Asian). Less touristy alternative to San Miguel.

**Best for:** Authentic experience, international options
**Try:** Peruvian stalls, seafood counters

### Mercado de Vallehermoso

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Chamberí |
| **Hours** | Mon-Sat 9am-2pm, 5pm-8pm |
| **Vibe** | Foodie paradise, local |
| **Price** | €€ |

**Why go:** Chamberí's foodie market. Mix of traditional vendors + excellent food stalls. Less discovered.

**Best for:** Serious food lovers, avoiding tourists
**Try:** Jamón, cheeses, local wine bars

### Mercado de Maravillas

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Tetuán (Bravo Murillo) |
| **Hours** | Mon-Sat 9am-2pm, 5pm-8pm |
| **Vibe** | Largest in Madrid, real shopping |
| **Price** | € |

**The facts:** Largest covered market in Madrid. Not trendy — this is where people actually buy groceries. Excellent prices.

**Best for:** Budget shopping, seeing real Madrid
**Skip if:** Want gastro experience or eating in

### Mercado de San Fernando

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Lavapiés |
| **Hours** | Tue-Sat 9am-3pm, 6pm-11pm (varies by stall) |
| **Vibe** | Local, community, alternative |
| **Price** | € |

**Hidden gem:** Half traditional market, half community space. Evening brings tapas, vermouth. Saturday mornings best.

**Best for:** Authentic neighborhood experience, Saturday morning tapas
**Try:** Cheese stalls, craft beer, wine bars

## Sunday Markets (Not Food Markets, But Have Food)

### El Rastro

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | La Latina (Ribera de Curtidores) |
| **Hours** | Sunday 9am-3pm |
| **What** | Massive flea market |

**Food situation:** Not a food market but La Latina is surrounded by tapas bars. Classic Sunday: Rastro → tapas → vermut.

**Eating tips:**
- Avoid restaurants inside Rastro (overpriced)
- Head to Cava Baja/Cava Alta for tapas after
- Vermut at 1pm is tradition

## Market Strategy

### For Tourists (Short Visit)

| Priority | Market | Why |
|----------|--------|-----|
| Must-do | Mercado de San Fernando | Authentic, good food |
| Skip | Mercado de San Miguel | Overpriced |
| If in Chueca | San Antón | Good balance |
| For tortilla | Mercado de la Paz | Casa Dani is legendary |

### For Residents

| Need | Market |
|------|--------|
| **Best value groceries** | Maravillas |
| **Upscale ingredients** | La Paz |
| **International + local** | Antón Martín |
| **Foodie experience** | Vallehermoso |
| **Saturday social** | San Fernando |

## Market Vocabulary

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| Carnicería | Butcher |
| Pescadería | Fishmonger |
| Frutería | Fruit/veg |
| Charcutería | Cured meats |
| Pollería | Poultry |

**Useful phrases:**
- "¿Qué tiene de bueno hoy?" — What's good today?
- "Póngame 200 gramos" — Give me 200 grams
- "¿Me lo corta?" — Can you slice it?
