# Visitor Tips — Madrid

## Transport Basics

### Payment
- **Tarjeta Multi** + Metrobús (10 trips) = best for tourists
- Or pay contactless (more expensive per trip)
- Airport metro has €3 supplement

### Best Apps
- **Citymapper** — Essential navigation
- **Metro de Madrid** — Official, service alerts
- **BiciMAD** — E-bike rentals

### Tips
- Madrid is very walkable — center fits in 1-hour diameter
- Avoid metro at rush hour (8-9:30am, 6-8pm)
- Taxi has €33 fixed rate to/from airport (refuse anything else)
- BiciMAD works well for short trips

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Eating at 7pm | Restaurants empty, tourists only. Eat at 2pm lunch, 9:30pm dinner |
| Plaza Mayor restaurants | Walk 2 blocks to La Latina |
| Mercado de San Miguel | Tourist trap. Go to San Fernando |
| Buying Oyster equivalent | Metrobús (10 trips) is simpler |
| Staying near Sol for quiet | Very noisy. Chamberí or Retiro instead |
| Scheduling tight | Spanish pace is relaxed, allow buffer |
| August visit | City empties, many closures, very hot |
| Tipping 20% | 5-10% is generous, nothing is also okay |

## Weather Reality

| Season | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| **Spring** | Best time. 20-25°C, occasional rain |
| **Summer** | Very hot. 35-40°C. Siesta makes sense |
| **Fall** | Excellent. 15-25°C |
| **Winter** | Cold but sunny. 5-12°C, dry |

**Pack:** Always layers. Temperature swings 15°C in a day.

## Safety Quick Guide

- **Main risk:** Pickpocketing (tourist areas)
- **Watch:** Metro, Sol, Gran Vía, El Rastro
- **Phone:** Front pocket, not back
- **Bag:** Cross-body, zip facing you
- **Scams:** Ignore petition signers, rose sellers

**Emergency:** 112 | Non-emergency police: 091

## Shopping Guide

### What to Buy
- **Jamón ibérico** (vacuum-packed for travel)
- **Olive oil** (quality stuff)
- **Vermouth** (Zarro, Yzaguirre)
- **Ceramics** (from shops, not tourist stands)
- **Leather goods** (Spanish leather is excellent)
- **Espadrilles** (traditional Spanish shoes)

### Where to Shop
| Type | Where |
|------|-------|
| **Souvenirs (quality)** | El Arco Artesanía, mercados |
| **Fashion** | Salamanca (luxury), Gran Vía (mainstream) |
| **Vintage** | Malasaña, El Rastro |
| **Food gifts** | La Barraca, El Corte Inglés food hall |

### Avoid
- Tourist shops near Sol (overpriced junk)
- "Authentic" items at El Rastro (mostly imports)

## Madrid Quirks

| Quirk | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| **Late everything** | Dinner at 10pm is normal |
| **Siesta** | Shops may close 2-5pm (less common now) |
| **Sunday closures** | Many shops closed, but Rastro is open |
| **August exodus** | Half the city leaves. Many closures |
| **Noise tolerance** | Spaniards are loud. Embrace it |
| **Asking for the check** | You must ask. They won't bring it |
| **Tap water** | Safe, but often asked "agua con gas o sin gas?" |

## Emergency Info

| Need | Contact |
|------|---------|
| **Any emergency** | 112 |
| **Police** | 091 |
| **Ambulance** | 061 |
| **Lost/stolen cards** | Cancel via app immediately |
| **Lost passport** | Police report → Embassy |
| **Hospital** | Go to Urgencias (ER), free for emergencies |

### Pharmacies (Farmacias)
- Green cross = pharmacy
- At least one open 24h per district
- Pharmacists can recommend for minor issues
- Many meds available without prescription

## Local Etiquette

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Greet with "Hola" or "Buenos días" | Jump straight to request |
| Say "Por favor" and "Gracias" | Be impatient |
| Take your time at meals | Rush servers |
| Dress reasonably neat | Wear beachwear (except beach day trips) |
| Join late dinner culture | Eat dinner at 6pm |
| Engage in small talk | Be overly formal |
