# Traditional Madrileño Food — Madrid

## Cocido Madrileño

THE dish of Madrid. Chickpea stew served in three "vuelcos" (pours): first the broth as soup with noodles, then the chickpeas and vegetables, finally the meats (chorizo, morcilla, beef, chicken, pork).

| Restaurant | Location | Price | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|-------|
| **Taberna La Bola** | Centro | €28-32 | Since 1870, wood-fire cooked, reservation essential |
| **La Barraca** | Recoletos | €25-30 | Excellent, less tourist |
| **Malacatín** | La Latina | €25-30 | Family-run, authentic |
| **Cruz Blanca de Vallecas** | Vallecas | €20-25 | Local favorite, value |
| **Lhardy** | Sol | €35-40 | Historic (1839), upscale |

**When:** Traditionally eaten on Wednesday (el cocido de los miércoles). Best in winter.

## Callos a la Madrileña

Tripe stew with chorizo, morcilla (blood sausage), and chickpeas in spicy paprika sauce. Sounds scary, tastes incredible.

| Restaurant | Location | Price | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|-------|
| **Taberna San Mamés** | Barrio de las Letras | €14-18 | Excellent callos |
| **Casa Alberto** | Huertas | €15-18 | Historic tavern (1827) |
| **Taberna La Bola** | Centro | €16-20 | Also famous for this |
| **La Casa del Abuelo** | Sol | €12-15 | More casual |

## Bocadillo de Calamares

Fried squid rings in a simple bread roll. Uniquely Madrid — a landlocked city obsessed with fried seafood in bread. Sounds weird, absolutely delicious.

| Bar | Location | Price | Notes |
|-----|----------|-------|-------|
| **Bar La Campana** | Plaza Mayor | €4-5 | Tourist area but authentic |
| **El Brillante** | Atocha | €4-5 | Local institution |
| **La Ideal** | Sol | €4-5 | Classic, crowded |
| **Los Galayos** | Plaza Mayor | €5-6 | Bigger portions |
| **La Barraca** | Near Sol | €5-6 | Upscale version |

**Tip:** Best eaten standing at the bar. Squeeze lemon, add hot sauce if offered.

## Huevos Rotos (Broken Eggs)

Fried eggs broken over fried potatoes, usually topped with jamón ibérico or chistorra. Simple perfection.

| Restaurant | Location | Price | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|-------|
| **Casa Lucio** | La Latina | €18-24 | Invented them. Celebrity spot |
| **La Casa de las Torrijas** | Centro | €12-16 | Good value |
| **Los Galayos** | Plaza Mayor | €14-18 | Tourist-friendly |
| **Lateral** | Multiple | €12-15 | Modern take |

**Must-try:** Casa Lucio's version — runny yolks mixing with crispy potatoes and salty ham.

## Tortilla Española

Spanish omelette with potato (and sometimes onion). Every bar has one. Quality varies enormously.

| Bar/Restaurant | Location | Price | Notes |
|----------------|----------|-------|-------|
| **Sylkar** | Malasaña | €4-6 (tapa) | Famous for their tortilla |
| **Casa Dani** | Mercado de la Paz | €10-12 | Award-winning, runny center |
| **Juana la Loca** | La Latina | €8-10 | Creative versions |
| **Cervecería 100 Montaditos** | Multiple | €2 | Cheap, decent |

**Debate:** Con cebolla (with onion) vs sin cebolla. Madrid is divided. Ask for preference.

## Churros con Chocolate

Fried dough sticks dipped in thick hot chocolate. Essential late-night or breakfast ritual.

| Place | Location | Price | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|-------|
| **Chocolatería San Ginés** | Sol | €5-7 | Since 1894, 24 hours, touristy but authentic |
| **Valor** | Multiple | €5-7 | Chain but quality |

## Asadores (Roast Houses)

Madrid is gateway to Castilla's roast tradition: cochinillo (suckling pig) and cordero (lamb).

| Restaurant | Specialty | Price | Notes |
|------------|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Botín** | Cochinillo | €30-40/person | "Oldest restaurant" claim, very touristy |
| **Casa Ciriaco** | Cordero | €25-35 | More local, less hyped |
| **La Barraca** | Both | €25-35 | Quality without tourist markup |

**Better option:** Day trip to Segovia for cochinillo (Mesón de Cándido) — much better value.

## Vermut Hour (El Vermut)

Sunday pre-lunch tradition. Stand at bar, drink vermut (vermouth), eat olives and conservas (tinned seafood).

| Vermutería | Location | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|
| **Bodega de la Ardosa** | Malasaña | Historic, draft vermut |
| **La Ardosa** | Chamberí | Excellent selection |
| **Casa Camacho** | Malasaña | Local legend |
| **Del Diego** | Chueca | Cocktails too |

**Timing:** Sunday 12pm-2pm, before late lunch.

## Where NOT to Eat Traditional Food

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **Plaza Mayor restaurants** | 2-3x markup, mediocre quality |
| **Gran Vía tourist spots** | Same |
| **Sol pedestrian area** | Overpriced chains |
| **Anywhere with photos on menu** | Usually tourist trap |
| **"Menu tourist" signs** | Designed to extract money |

**Instead:** Walk 2 blocks away from any monument. Quality up, prices down.
