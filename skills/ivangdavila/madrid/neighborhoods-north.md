# North Madrid Neighborhoods

## 1. Chamberí

**Best for**: Families, professionals seeking classic Madrid, foodies

**Character**: Classic madrileño neighborhood with elegant 19th-century buildings. Strong local identity. Excellent restaurants and traditional shops. Not trendy, just genuinely nice.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,400–1,900 | 2BR: €1,800–2,500 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Very safe, beautiful architecture, excellent food scene, authentic Madrid, good schools
**Cons**: Expensive for what it is, limited nightlife, can feel sleepy

**Transit**: Alonso Cano, Iglesia, Ríos Rosas, Quevedo, Bilbao
**Must-do**: Andén 0 (ghost metro station), Mercado de Vallehermoso, vermut at Santamaría

### Sub-areas within Chamberí:
- **Alonso Cano**: Very residential, families
- **Trafalgar**: Closer to Malasaña, younger
- **Gaztambide**: Near universities, students
- **Vallehermoso**: Foodie haven, excellent market

---

## 2. Tetuán

**Best for**: Budget-conscious professionals, immigrants, those wanting value

**Character**: Working-class neighborhood rapidly gentrifying. Large immigrant community (Dominican, Moroccan, Chinese). Gritty but improving. Excellent value for central location.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,000–1,400 | 2BR: €1,300–1,800 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Affordable, great transport (Nuevos Ministerios), diverse food, up-and-coming
**Cons**: Less safe perception (though improving), aesthetically mixed, gentrification tensions

**Transit**: Tetuán, Estrecho, Alvarado, Cuatro Caminos, Nuevos Ministerios
**Must-do**: Mercado de Tetuán, Dominican restaurants, explore side streets

### Sub-areas within Tetuán:
- **Bellas Vistas**: Near metro, improving
- **Cuatro Caminos**: Commercial hub
- **Castillejos**: More residential
- **Valdeacederas**: Working class, affordable

---

## 3. Moncloa-Aravaca

**Best for**: University students, affluent families, academics

**Character**: University area (Complutense, ICAI) meets wealthy residential. Parque del Oeste is beautiful. Mix of student energy and family calm depending on sub-area.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,200–1,700 | 2BR: €1,600–2,200 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Near universities, green spaces, good schools, safe, Casa de Campo access
**Cons**: Split personality (student vs family), some areas far from center

**Transit**: Moncloa, Argüelles, Ciudad Universitaria, Príncipe Pío
**Must-do**: Teleférico to Casa de Campo, Templo de Debod sunset, Parque del Oeste

### Sub-areas:
- **Argüelles**: Urban, young, near Malasaña
- **Ciudad Universitaria**: Campus area, very student-focused
- **Aravaca**: Wealthy suburb, villas, families
- **Casa de Campo**: Near the massive park

---

## 4. Hortaleza

**Best for**: Families seeking modern housing, tech workers (near business park)

**Character**: Outer-ring residential area with modern developments. Near business parks and IFEMA convention center. Practical rather than charming.

| Rent | 1BR: €900–1,300 | 2BR: €1,200–1,700 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Modern apartments, parking, near business parks, good for families
**Cons**: Disconnected from central Madrid, limited culture, car helpful

**Transit**: Pinar de Chamartín, Mar de Cristal, Aeropuerto (metro to airport)
**Must-do**: IFEMA events, local parks, Parque Juan Carlos I

---

## 5. Fuencarral-El Pardo

**Best for**: Nature lovers, families, those wanting semi-rural feel

**Character**: Northern edge of Madrid. El Pardo is semi-rural with forests. Montecarmelo and Mirasierra are modern developments. Best of both worlds for some.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,000–1,400 | 2BR: €1,300–1,800 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Nature access (Monte de El Pardo), modern housing, quieter, parking
**Cons**: Far from center, car recommended, limited nightlife

**Transit**: Paco de Lucía, Montecarmelo (metro limited), bus routes
**Must-do**: Monte de El Pardo walks, Palacio de El Pardo (Franco's residence)

---

## 6. Barajas

**Best for**: Airport workers, frequent travelers, those wanting space

**Character**: Airport district but also has village-like old Barajas. Modern developments near airport. Very safe, surprisingly pleasant historic core.

| Rent | 1BR: €950–1,300 | 2BR: €1,200–1,600 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Very safe, airport proximity, old Barajas is charming, affordable
**Cons**: Far from center, can feel isolated, plane noise in some areas

**Transit**: Barajas (Line 8 to airport), Alameda de Osuna
**Must-do**: Old Barajas village, Feria de Madrid (IFEMA), Parque Juan Carlos I
