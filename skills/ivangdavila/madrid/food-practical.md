# Food Practical Guide — Madrid

## Dietary Requirements

### Vegetarian

**Good news:** More options than ever. Tortilla, pimientos de padrón, gazpacho, salmorejo, patatas bravas are naturally vegetarian.

**Challenge:** Traditional Madrid cuisine is meat-heavy. Many "vegetable" dishes cooked with ham.

**Always ask:** "¿Lleva jamón/carne?" (Does it have ham/meat?)

| Vegetarian-Friendly Restaurants | Location | Notes |
|--------------------------------|----------|-------|
| **Levél Veggie Bistro** | Malasaña | Fully vegetarian |
| **Vega** | Centro | Vegan restaurant |
| **B13** | Centro | Vegan tapas |
| **Rayén** | Malasaña | Veggie-focused |
| **El Perro y La Galleta** | Salamanca | Good veggie options |

**Safe bets everywhere:**
- Tortilla española (unless made with ham)
- Patatas bravas
- Pimientos de padrón
- Gazpacho / Salmorejo (cold soups)
- Pan con tomate
- Mushroom dishes (champiñones)

### Vegan

More challenging. Egg and dairy prevalent. Ask: "¿Es vegano?" or "Sin huevo, sin leche, sin queso"

| Vegan Restaurants | Location | Notes |
|-------------------|----------|-------|
| **Vega** | Centro | Fully vegan |
| **B13** | Centro | Vegan tapas |
| **Pizzi & Dixie** | Malasaña | Vegan options |
| **Freedom Cakes** | Malasaña | Vegan bakery |

**Watch out for:**
- Tortilla (eggs)
- Croquetas (cream, egg)
- Most sauces (may contain dairy)
- Bread sometimes brushed with butter

### Gluten-Free

Spanish cuisine uses less bread than others, but gluten is everywhere in tapas (croquetas, fried things, bread with everything).

**Always ask:** "¿Tiene gluten?" or "Soy celíaco/a"

| Gluten-Free Options | Notes |
|--------------------|-------|
| **Grilled meats** | Usually safe |
| **Seafood** | Grilled/steamed safe, NOT fried |
| **Huevos rotos** | Potatoes + eggs (ask about oil) |
| **Padrones/pimientos** | Safe |
| **Jamón** | Safe |

**Restaurants with GF menus:** Most modern restaurants accommodate. Traditional bars less so.

**Celiac Association Spain:** celiacos.org has restaurant guides

### Halal

Growing halal scene thanks to Muslim immigrant community.

| Area | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Lavapiés** | Many halal options (Moroccan, Pakistani, Bangladeshi) |
| **Usera** | Some halal Chinese |
| **Tetuán** | Halal butchers and restaurants |

### Kosher

Limited but available.

| Restaurant | Location | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|
| **Alfredo's Barbacoa** | Multiple | Certified kosher |
| **Centro Sefarad** | Centro | Community center with events |

### Allergies

"Tengo alergia a..." (I'm allergic to...)

Spanish law requires allergen menus. Ask for "la carta de alérgenos."

## Tipping Guide

| Situation | Standard Tip |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Casual bar** | Nothing or round up |
| **Tapas restaurant** | Round up or 5% |
| **Nice restaurant** | 5-10% |
| **Fine dining** | 10% (check if service included) |
| **Coffee** | Keep coins |
| **Delivery** | €1-2 |

**Reality:** Tipping is appreciated, not expected. Servers earn decent wages. Not tipping = not rude.

## Eating Hours (Critical!)

### Typical Madrid Schedule

| Meal | Time | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Desayuno** | 8-10am | Light: coffee + tostada |
| **Almuerzo** | 11am-12pm | Mid-morning snack (optional) |
| **Comida** | 2-4pm | MAIN meal |
| **Merienda** | 6-7pm | Light snack |
| **Cena** | 9-11pm | Lighter than lunch |
| **After dinner** | Midnight+ | More tapas/drinks |

### Restaurant Hours

| Type | Opens for Lunch | Opens for Dinner |
|------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Traditional** | 1-1:30pm | 8:30-9pm |
| **Modern/international** | 1pm | 8pm |
| **Tapas bars** | All day (many) | Varies |

**Mistakes tourists make:**
- Arriving at 7pm for dinner (restaurant closed or empty)
- Eating lunch at 12pm (only tourists there)
- Expecting quick dinner at 10pm (dinner just starting)

## Ordering Like a Local

### At a Tapas Bar

1. Stand at bar or find table
2. Get attention: "¡Perdona!" or eye contact
3. Order drinks first
4. Order 2-3 tapas to start, more later
5. Share everything
6. Don't rush — enjoying time is the point
7. Ask for "la cuenta" when done

### Useful Phrases

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| "¿Qué tienen de tapas?" | What tapas do you have? |
| "¿Qué me recomienda?" | What do you recommend? |
| "Para compartir" | To share |
| "¿Nos pone otra ronda?" | Another round? |
| "La cuenta, por favor" | The bill, please |
| "¿Aceptan tarjeta?" | Do you take card? |

## Money-Saving Tips

| Tip | Savings |
|-----|---------|
| **Menú del día** | 3 courses €12-16 vs €30+ à la carte |
| **Caña not pint** | Smaller = cheaper, stays cold |
| **Stand at bar** | Often cheaper than table |
| **Avoid Plaza Mayor** | 50% markup |
| **El Tigre free tapas** | Free tapas with every drink |
| **Lunch > dinner** | Same food, lower prices |
| **Markets over restaurants** | Better value |

## Summary for AI Agent

For food practical questions:

1. **Vegetarian:** Possible but ask about ham. Vega, B13 for dedicated options
2. **Timing:** Lunch 2-4pm, dinner 9-11pm. Don't be early.
3. **Tipping:** 0-10% is normal. Not tipping isn't rude
4. **Best value:** Menú del día for lunch
5. **Booking:** Casual = walk in. Popular spots = book ahead
6. **Phrases:** "¿Qué me recomienda?" + "La cuenta, por favor" covers 80%
