# Safety Guide — Madrid

## Overall Assessment

Madrid is one of the safest major European capitals. Violent crime against tourists is extremely rare. Main concerns are petty theft (pickpocketing, distraction scams) and occasional phone snatching. Exercise normal urban awareness.

**Comparison:** Safer than Barcelona, Paris, Rome, or London for street crime. Among the safest major capitals in Europe.

## Crime by Type

### Pickpocketing (Most Common)

| Hotspots | How It Happens | Prevention |
|----------|----------------|------------|
| Metro (Line 1, Sol-Gran Vía) | Rush hour crowding | Bag in front, zip closed |
| Puerta del Sol | Tourist distraction | Stay alert to approaches |
| Plaza Mayor | Restaurant distraction | Bag on lap or hook |
| El Rastro (Sundays) | Browsing crowds | Cross-body bag, front zip |
| Gran Vía | Shopping distraction | Don't flash expensive items |

### Phone Snatching (Rising)

| Method | Where | Prevention |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Moped snatch | Near curbs, crossings | Phone away when walking |
| Grab and run | Terraza (outdoor seating) | Never put phone on table |
| Distraction | Asking for directions | Keep interactions brief |

**Tip:** Apple/Samsung theft protection features recommended.

### Scams (Tourist-Targeting)

| Scam | How It Works | Response |
|------|--------------|----------|
| **Fake petition** | Deaf charity clipboard → pickpocket accomplice | Decline, keep walking |
| **Rose/bracelet sellers** | Force item on you → demand payment | Firmly refuse, don't accept |
| **Shell game** | Street gambling near Sol → rigged | Never play, crowd are shills |
| **Taxi overcharge** | Refusing meter, inflated quote | Insist on meter or leave |
| **ATM helpers** | Offers help → shoulder surfing | Decline, cover PIN |
| **Fake police** | Ask to check wallet → steal | Ask for credentials, offer to go to station |
| **Apartment scams** | Pay deposit before viewing | Never pay without seeing |

### Violent Crime (Context)

- Very rare against tourists
- Most incidents are domestic or gang-related
- Occurs in specific areas, usually late night
- Does NOT affect normal tourist/resident experience

| ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Safe | Malasaña, Chueca, La Latina, Arganzuela, Chamartín |

**Note:** Even "caution" areas are fine during daytime. Issues arise late night, away from main streets.

## District Safety Rankings (2025 Police Data)

### Safest Districts (Lowest Crime Rates)
1. **Vicálvaro** — Lowest crime rate in Madrid
2. **Retiro** — Very safe, family-friendly
3. **Moratalaz** — Quiet, residential
4. **Barajas** — Safe, family-oriented
5. **Moncloa-Aravaca** — Affluent, university area

### Higher Crime Districts (Still Safe, More Caution)
- **Centro** — Highest volume (tourism + density, not danger)
- **Carabanchel** — Large, varies by neighborhood
- **Puente de Vallecas** — Working class, some areas rougher
- **Villaverde** — Industrial history, some problem spots

**Perspective:** Even "higher crime" Madrid districts are safer than average neighborhoods in many other capitals.

## Night Safety

### What Changes After Dark

| Time | Considerations |
|------|----------------|
| 8pm-midnight | Very safe, busy streets, active nightlife |
| Midnight-3am | Normal nightlife hours, stick to populated areas |
| 3am-6am | Quieter, use taxi/Uber for longer distances |

### Areas to Avoid Late Night

- Deserted metro stations
- Industrial areas (everywhere)
- Isolated parks (Casa de Campo interiors, Retiro interiors)
- Very quiet residential streets alone
- Specific blocks in Lavapiés, Vallecas (ask locals)

### Safe Late-Night Areas

- Malasaña, Chueca, La Latina, Huertas — Active until 4-5am
- Gran Vía — Always people around
- Salamanca — Safe but quiet late

## Women's Safety

**Overall:** Madrid is safe for solo female travelers. Catcalling exists but is less aggressive than some countries.

| Consideration | Advice |
|---------------|--------|
| Solo dining/bars | Completely normal, no stigma |
| Public transport | Very safe, even late |
| Walking alone | Fine in busy/lit areas |
| Harassment | Rare, bystanders typically intervene |
| Drink safety | Standard precautions apply |

**Resources:**
- Emergency: 112
- Women's helpline (24h): 016 (free, confidential)
- Police for gender violence: 091

## Emergency Numbers

| Number | Service | When to Use |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **112** | General emergency | Any emergency (police, fire, ambulance) |
| **091** | National Police | Crime, emergencies |
| **092** | Local Police (Policía Municipal) | Local issues |
| **061** | Medical emergency | Health emergencies |
| **016** | Gender violence helpline | Free, confidential, 24h |
| **024** | Suicide prevention | 24h support |

**Hospital Emergency (Urgencias):** Go to any hospital. Treatment is free for emergencies regardless of insurance.

**Lost passport:** Go to your embassy (most in Salamanca/Castellana area).

## Practical Safety Tips

### Daily Habits

| Tip | Why |
|-----|-----|
| **Bag across body, zip facing you** | Harder to snatch or unzip |
| **Phone in front pocket** | Back pocket = easy target |
| **Don't flash expensive items** | Attracts attention |
| **Be aware at ATMs** | Cover PIN, use indoor ATMs |
| **Trust your instincts** | If uncomfortable, leave |

### If Something Happens

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Pickpocketed** | Cancel cards (app), police report, get denuncia for insurance |
| **Phone snatched** | Mark as lost (Find My), police report, denuncia |
| **Mugged** | Don't resist, give items, call 112 |
| **Lost/stolen passport** | Police report → embassy |
| **Medical emergency** | 112, or go to nearest Urgencias |

### Getting a Denuncia (Police Report)

Required for insurance claims. Two options:

1. **In person:** Go to any Comisaría (police station)
2. **Online:** denuncias.policia.es (in Spanish)

Bring: ID, description of incident/items stolen

## Terrorism Context

Spain has experienced terrorism but current threat level is moderate. Madrid has visible but unobtrusive security:
- Police presence at major attractions
- Bag checks at some venues
- Bollards protecting pedestrian areas

**Practical impact:** None. Security is effective and doesn't impede daily life.

## Drugs

Cannabis is decriminalized for personal use in private spaces. However:
- Consumption in public is fined
- Selling is illegal
- "Cannabis clubs" exist in legal gray area
- Street dealers (parks, nightlife areas) should be avoided
