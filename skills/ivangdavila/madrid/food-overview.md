# Food Overview — Madrid

Madrid is a food paradise. Unlike Barcelona (Mediterranean) or Bilbao (Basque), Madrid's cuisine is hearty, interior Spanish — heavy on meat, stews, and fried things. But as the capital, it also hosts the best of every Spanish region plus excellent international options.

## What Madrid Does Well

| Cuisine | Why | Best Areas |
|---------|-----|------------|
| **Castilian** | The local cuisine: cocido, callos, roasts | La Latina, Centro |
| **Tapas** | Every bar has something | La Latina, Malasaña |
| **Seafood** | Best transport = freshest fish inland | Chamberí, Salamanca |
| **Asturian** | Sidra + fabada community | Chamberí, Argüelles |
| **Galician** | Pulpo, empanadas, great seafood | Throughout |
| **Basque** | Pintxos bars | Chamberí, Salamanca |
| **Chinese** | Largest Chinatown in Spain | Usera |
| **Peruvian** | Massive community | Tetuán, Lavapiés |
| **Venezuelan** | Arepas everywhere | Throughout |

**Key insight:** Madrid's superpower is variety. Every Spanish region + Latin America + international.

## The Menú del Día (Greatest Value in Europe)

**What:** 3-course lunch (appetizer + main + dessert/coffee) + bread + drink
**Price:** €12-18 typically
**When:** Weekday lunch, usually 1pm-4pm
**Where:** Almost every non-tourist restaurant

**Example:**
- Primer plato: Ensalada mixta or gazpacho
- Segundo plato: Merluza or secreto ibérico  
- Postre: Flan or café
- Bebida: Wine, beer, or water
- **Total: €14**

**Tips:**
- Best value meal in Europe
- Quality varies wildly — ask locals
- Tourist areas often don't offer it
- Best ones have chalkboard outside, locals inside

## Timing (Very Important!)

| Meal | Time | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Breakfast** | 8-10am | Coffee + tostada or churros |
| **Mid-morning** | 11am-12pm | Coffee break, maybe pintxo |
| **Lunch** | 2-4pm | MAIN meal. Everything closes. |
| **Merienda** | 6-7pm | Coffee/snack bridge to dinner |
| **Dinner** | 9-11pm | Lighter than lunch usually |
| **Late night** | After midnight | Tapas, churros |

**Warning:** 
- Restaurants often don't open for dinner until 8:30-9pm
- Eating at 7pm = eating with other tourists
- Lunch at 1pm = empty restaurant
- Prime time: Lunch 2pm, Dinner 9:30pm

## Tipping Culture

| Situation | Tip |
|-----------|-----|
| **Casual tapas/bar** | Round up or nothing |
| **Restaurant** | 5-10% if good service |
| **Fine dining** | 10% (service sometimes included) |
| **Coffee** | Round up |
| **Taxi** | Round up |

**Note:** Tipping is appreciated but not expected like US. No tipping = not rude.
