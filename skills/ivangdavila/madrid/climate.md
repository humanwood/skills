# Climate Guide — Madrid

## Overview

Madrid has a **continental Mediterranean climate** with extreme temperature swings. Very hot, dry summers and cold, sunny winters. The saying goes: "Nueve meses de invierno, tres de infierno" (nine months of winter, three of hell) — slightly exaggerated but captures the extremes.

**Key characteristics:**
- Hot summers (35-40°C peaks in July-August)
- Cold winters (often below 5°C, occasional snow)
- Low humidity year-round
- 300+ sunny days/year
- Big day-night temperature swings (10-15°C)

## Season Details

### Spring (March - May)

**Best time to visit.** Pleasant temperatures, occasional rain showers, city comes alive.

| What to Expect | What to Pack |
|----------------|--------------|
| 15-25°C days | Layers (mornings cold, afternoons warm) |
| Occasional rain | Light jacket |
| Sunny most days | Sunglasses |
| Warm by late May | Short sleeves for May |

**Highlights:**
- Terrazas (outdoor terraces) open
- San Isidro festival (May 15)
- Excellent for walking/sightseeing

### Summer (June - August)

**Very hot.** July-August can be brutal (35-40°C). Early June and late August more bearable.

| What to Expect | What to Pack |
|----------------|--------------|
| 30-40°C days | Light, breathable clothing |
| Very dry | Sunscreen (essential) |
| Cool evenings (18-20°C) | Light layer for AC/evening |
| UV very high | Hat, sunglasses |

**Tips:**
- Sightseeing: Early morning (before 11am) or evening (after 7pm)
- Siesta: 2-5pm is dead time (shops close, locals rest)
- Hydrate constantly
- AC in restaurants/museums = your friend
- Rooftop bars come alive at night

**August Warning:** Half of Madrid leaves for vacation. Many local shops/restaurants close. Touristy areas stay open but city feels empty.

### Autumn (September - November)

**Excellent.** September is warm but comfortable. October beautiful. November gets cold.

| What to Expect | What to Pack |
|----------------|--------------|
| September: 25-28°C | Summer clothes + light jacket |
| October: 18-22°C | Layers, light jacket |
| November: 10-15°C | Warmer jacket, layers |
| More rain than summer | Umbrella (just in case) |

**Highlights:**
- Perfect temperatures for walking
- Terrazas still open until late October
- Harvest food (mushrooms, game)
- Cultural season begins

### Winter (December - February)

**Cold but sunny.** Dry cold, not damp like Northern Europe. Snow rare but possible.

| What to Expect | What to Pack |
|----------------|--------------|
| 5-12°C days | Warm coat |
| Near 0°C nights | Layers, scarf, gloves |
| Sunny most days | Sunglasses (yes, winter sun) |
| Occasional frost/ice | Watch for ice mornings |

**Tips:**
- Indoor heating is good
- Christmas decorations beautiful (Gran Vía lights)
- New Year's Eve at Puerta del Sol (grapes tradition)
- January sales (rebajas) — great shopping

## Weather Phenomena

### The Madrid Heat Island

Central Madrid is 2-5°C warmer than outskirts. Concrete retains heat. Evening walks along Madrid Río or in Retiro provide relief.

### Temperature Swings

Morning can be 15°C, afternoon 30°C. **Always layer.** Madrileños carry jackets even in summer for morning/evening/AC.

### The "Gota Fría" (Cold Drop)

Occasional intense rainfall events, especially September-October. Usually brief but torrential. Can cause flash flooding.

### Filomena (The Exception)

January 2021 saw historic snowfall (Storm Filomena). Extremely rare — don't expect it, but it can happen.

## What to Pack by Season

### Spring Visit (Mar-May)
- Layers (t-shirt + jacket)
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Light rain jacket
- Sunglasses
- Sunscreen (from April)

### Summer Visit (Jun-Aug)
- Light, breathable clothing
- Hat (essential)
- Sunscreen SPF 50+
- Sunglasses
- Refillable water bottle
- Light cardigan for AC

### Autumn Visit (Sep-Nov)
- Layers
- Light to medium jacket
- Umbrella
- Comfortable shoes
- Scarf for November

### Winter Visit (Dec-Feb)
- Warm coat
- Layers (heated indoors)
- Scarf, gloves, hat
- Sunglasses (winter sun!)
- Warm but walkable shoes

## Best Times to Visit

| Purpose | Best Months |
|---------|-------------|
| **Overall** | May, September-October |
| **Budget travel** | January-February (except holidays) |
| **Festivals** | May (San Isidro), December (Christmas) |
| **Art/culture** | Fall/Winter (exhibition season) |
| **Nightlife** | Spring/Fall (comfortable terraza weather) |
| **Families** | April-May, September-October |

### Avoid If Possible
- **August**: Too hot + everything closed
- **August 15 weekend**: Entire city shuts down
- **December 31-January 1**: Very crowded, sold out

## Air Quality

Madrid occasionally has air quality alerts, especially in winter (temperature inversions trap pollution) and summer heat waves. Check AQICN.org if sensitive.

Low emission zone (Madrid 360) has improved air quality significantly.
