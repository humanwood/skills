# Weather Guide — Paris

## Overview

Paris has a temperate oceanic climate. Expect grey skies and rain year-round, but rarely extreme temperatures. Summers can be hot; winters are cold but rarely freezing.

**Best times to visit:** May-June, September-October
**Avoid:** August (locals leave, many closures), February (grey, cold)

## What to Pack

### Spring (Mar-May)
- Layers (t-shirt + sweater + light jacket)
- Light rain jacket
- Comfortable walking shoes
- One warm outfit for cold days

### Summer (Jun-Aug)
- Light clothing
- Sunglasses, sunscreen
- Light jacket for evenings
- Water bottle (fountains everywhere)

### Fall (Sep-Nov)
- Layers (warmer)
- Waterproof jacket
- Umbrella
- Scarf

### Winter (Dec-Feb)
- Warm coat
- Scarf, hat, gloves
- Warm boots
- Layers for indoors (heated)

## Weather Myths

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| "Always raining" | 10 rain days/month = not constant, often drizzle |
| "Cold winters" | Rarely below freezing, just grey |
| "Hot summers" | Can be 35°C+ heat waves, AC rare |
| "April is spring" | Can be cold, variable |

---

## Heat Waves

Recent summers (2019, 2022, 2023) had heat waves of 38-40°C.

**Coping strategies:**
- Air conditioning rare — book AC hotel if summer
- Cool spots: Museums (Louvre is cool), Catacombs, cinemas
- Parks: Jardin des Plantes, Tuileries (shade)
- Evening outings better than midday
